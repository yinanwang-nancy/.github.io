Motivation & Goal

- Design an OPV with a special study on the adaptability to future machinery and fuels.

- Explore how propulsion, payload, Structures, Systems, Access & voids and margins affect the overal ship design.

Design Process

User Requirement

- Starting from User Requirements Document (URD).
- Identify realisable statement of requirements: Payloads (weapons, sensors, hangers...), speeds, endurances, etc.
- Baseline Staff Target (BST)
- System Requirements Document (SRD)

Main Design Process

- Initial Sizing: Based on URD, first assume a typical set of hullform parameters; determine an estimate for the required size of the vessel; then use the sizing spreasheet tool to iterate until the design is balanced.
- Parametric Survey: Explore the effects of varying hullform parameters on the design; Select a suitable set of hullform parameters based on appropriate design constraints.
- Layout: Determine a suitable arrangement of compartments.
- Stability Analysis: Calculate the centre of gravity (COG) and stability characteristics.
- Propulsion analysis: Define the propulsion system design; Estimate the maximum speed of the vessel from the installed power, and the range attainable at cruise speed.
- Costing: Estimate the procurement cost of the vessel.
