Motivation & Goal

The focus of this project was on resistance and seakeeping evaluations of the Series 60 ship model using the Data Acquisition System designed for the UCL Towing Tank. As the UCL Towing Tank is comparatively small in size, my main goal was to investigate the effect of tank size on ship testing results compared to other Towing Tank facilities.

- Methods: Develop a measurement protocol for parametric and resistance analysis of vessels;

- Contribution: Write an academic report based on experimental and modelling results;

- Impact: Present work and disseminate information to academic and industrial partners.

Remarkable Milestones

Surface Sea tests

- 3D ship hull design and CFD analysis of the surface ship model overcoming waves and winds in Maxsurf and Paramarine;

- Conducted experimental tests in UCL Towing Tank to evaluate resitance and seakeeping performances of the Series 60 ship model;

- Carried out strain gauge calibration, model ballasting, turbulence stimulator setup, etc.

Sub sea design

- Designed the pressure hull for the UCL ROV using CATIA and 3D printing techniques;

- CFD analysis to simulate the surrounding fluid flow and evaluate its underwater manoeuvrability;

- Dynamic ROV model and control system design in Matlab/Simulink (PID control);

- Control and path planning software of the physical hardware in ArduSUB/C++;
